Your Guide to Credit-Card Protections

Credit cards include powerful consumer protections: purchase protection, extended warranties, fraud liability limits and dispute rights. This guide explains how they actually work.

Compare protection-heavy cards

Why Credit-Card Protections Matter

Many people choose cards based on rewards or travel perks — but consumer protections are often more valuable. Protections help when items arrive damaged, get stolen, break early, or when merchants refuse refunds.

These benefits vary across issuers and networks, but they typically follow a consistent structure: purchase protection, extended warranty, fraud liability, and dispute rights.

Types of Card Protections

1. Purchase Protection

Covers theft or accidental damage shortly after purchase (commonly 60–120 days). Limits depend on the card and network. Not all items qualify.

2. Extended Warranty

Extends the manufacturer’s warranty by 1–2 years on eligible items. Often applies to electronics and appliances; exclusions vary.

3. Zero-Liability & Fraud Monitoring

Credit cards protect you from unauthorized charges. As long as you report fraud promptly, you typically owe nothing.

4. Travel Protections

Some cards include baggage, delay, cancellation or rental-car insurance. These vary heavily by issuer.

How Chargebacks & Disputes Work

Chargebacks exist to protect consumers when merchants fail to deliver goods or services. The bank temporarily reverses the transaction while investigating.

Common dispute reasons include: item not received, defective items, unauthorized transactions, incorrect charges, subscription cancellations and more.

Documentation is key — receipts, screenshots, correspondence and delivery proof all strengthen a dispute case.
